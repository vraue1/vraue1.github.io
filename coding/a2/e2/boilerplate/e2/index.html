I redesigned this webpage based on the idea of simplicity. In my experience on recipe websites there is a chaotic amount of information. I wanted to keep this webpage minimal and uncluttered with only information that I believe is necessary to determine before you cook something and to help you decide what you want to cook. I think you’d generally need to know the length of time it takes to make and the quantity, as well as the dietary label. The information is displayed through sizing and spacing that does not take too long to scroll to the bottom but also allows the user the discrete action of scrolling and clicking so that they’re engaged. The colours contrast and stand out clearly against the clean white backdrop for simplicity and aesthetic pleasure. The link to each recipe stands out clearly as this is where the user will need the most obvious direction to if they select a recipe. The specific categories in the filter were chosen to streamline the user’s selection process in a way that tailors to a wide and general population. The filters were separated into main meals and desserts as a user will probably already know which of these they want to navigate to prior. Dietary selection is highly relevant as people also know prior to browsing whether they are interested in a meat based or vegetarian recipe and searching based on their own dietary requirements removes unnecessary scrolling for them. Cuisine is based on interest and is filtered to navigate for more flavour profile based navigation.
